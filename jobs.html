Head of Product - Dojo

March 2016 - September 2016
After starting off as an Android developer I went on to lead the development team. My priority was to introduce a structured work methodology into the team, so as the team could double in size, increase the technological complexity, the work efficiency and be brought into its next generation.

To name a few, this included daily stand-ups, fortnightly retrospectives, fortnightly one-to-ones, knowledge sharing + pairing, application championing, git discipline, strict coding standards, microservice paradigms, feature roadmap paradigms, testing, testing, testing, structuring team-member onboarding and a glorious 9 month Gantt chart.

An analytics heavy system was to come to fruition. Giving users live suggestions based on the users habits and like-users tastes. By means of injecting content within the users app session and via push notifications based on time of day and user location. The complex infrastructure meant a well documented and understood architecture was vital. Consulting team members individually and as a group to make sure we knew we were making the best deciisoons possible and iterating quickly to changes in design without hindrance to the overall project. The team became solid, excelling both technically and culturally.

Bringing the best out of the designers by opening up the communication amongst the development team so as the features had the best possible tech considerations early on in the design process.

Whilst leading the team I developed a great understanding of AWS ecosystems, tying our EC2 + Lambda microservices together. Monitoring and logging was made possible via NewRelic, in house slackbots and Dockerising our applications.

Dojo is a lifestyle app for helping millenials find stuff do to in their cities. Currently based in London and Paris it has 750k downloads on Android and iOS.

Android Developer - Dojo

January 2015 - March 2016
Since day 1 I have been the sole Android developer at Dojo. It can be found here. Boasting over 100k downloads and a rating of 4.5 it uses Androids latest technologies to remain smooth and sleak. Feature parity between iOS and Android was maintained whilst implementing some Android specific quirks to keep it feeling like an Android app.

RxJava - RxJava introduces functional reactive programming to Android. Means I never have to mutter the words A-Sync-Task ever again.

Kotlin - After introducing Kotlin to the Android source code last Autumn. Is a beautiful language that removes some of Javas nuissances and introduces some modern language features. My favourite features would be inline functions, null-safety handling, smart casting, string templates, singleton handling, extensions functions. To namy a few. Coding in Java isn't an issue, I just had the privilege at Dojo of being able to implement an interoperable language into the source and too advantage of that.

MVP - MVP architecture throughout the app using the Mosby library ensures the codebase is maintainable, clean and testable.

99.9% crash free - Cause that's how it should be.
