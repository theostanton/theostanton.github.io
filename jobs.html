Head of Product - Dojo

March 2016 - September 2016
After starting off as an Android developer I was promoted to lead the development team. Managing the iOS, Android, Web, Backend, Analytics and Internal Website development. My priority was to introduce a structured work methodology into the team, so as the team could double in size, increase the technological complexity, the work efficiency and be brought into its next generation.

Management - Introduced daily stand-ups, fortnightly retrospectives, fortnightly one-to-ones, knowledge sharing + pairing, application championing, git discipline, strict coding standards, microservice paradigms, feature roadmap paradigms, testing, testing, testing, structuring team-member onboarding and a glorious 9 month Gantt chart. The team became solid, excelling both technically and culturally.

Culture - Culture is paramount. For my two years at Dojo I looked forward to each and every day, not once glancing at the clock. It was essential that this transpired to the rest of the development team. Transparency and candour are important to me, leaving nothing unsaid. Everybody contributes, everbody's opinion is valued and so everybody knows they are performing at their utmost, learning along the way. If you're working with me you will enjoy it.

Design - When using any app is it possible to decipher the quality of communication between the tech and design team. Leading the tech and design teams I made sure that communication was seamless, enabling qualities from both realms to shine through. This communication is to come from the team members too, in developer roles I am sure to communicate clearly to the design team so as they are always aware of what is possible.

Analytics - On becoming Head of Product a major feature I designed and pushed was an improved analytics system. Creating an in-house analytics data warehouse that fed live recommendations and suggestions to users, as well as bringing the best possibly stats to the content team, design team and business owners. I hired 2 specialist backend developers and a skilled data scientist specifically for the purpose. The project was complex and involved each member of the development team. The success of the project came down to smart design and architecture, consulting the relevant team members, concise documentation and careful structuring and planning of the teams workloads and dependencies. Unfortunately I finished at Dojo before it came to production, I took away a great deal of knowledge along the journey.

Google + Apple - Meetings with the Play Store team at Google and the App Store team at Apple were common. Conferences at both thef Google and Apple campuses to keep on top of what the platforms have to offer.

AWS - Whilst leading the team I developed a great understanding of AWS ecosystems, tying our EC2 + Lambda microservices together. Monitoring and logging was made possible via NewRelic, in house slackbots and Dockerising our applications.

Dojo - Dojo is a lifestyle app for helping millenials find stuff do to in their cities. Currently based in London and Paris it has 750k downloads on Android and iOS.

My time at Dojo was a fantastic experience. Evolving as a programmer, I became a generalist developer with experience in full stack, analytics and design. Understanding the production cycle I know have refined foresight at all stages of the process. Learning to lead a development team, getting the best out of each team member ensuring everyone goes home satisfied with their day, proud of their work and enjoyed themselves. Amongst these explicit skills above and other experience based skills, common sense prevailed in this role readin and adapting to evolving situations, working in an agile methodology, so as each member of the team harnesses their talents.

Android Developer - Dojo

January 2015 - March 2016
Since day 1 I have been the sole Android developer at Dojo. It can be found here. Boasting over 100k downloads, a rating of 4.5, #4 Best app of 2015 according to Google Play Store and featured countless times. It uses Androids latest technologies to remain smooth and sleak. Feature parity between iOS and Android was maintained whilst implementing some Android specific quirks to keep it feeling like an Android app.

RxJava - RxJava introduces functional reactive programming to Android. Means I never have to mutter the words A-Sync-Task ever again.

Kotlin - After introducing Kotlin to the Android source code last Autumn. Is a beautiful language that removes some of Javas nuissances and introduces some modern language features. My favourite features would be inline functions, null-safety handling, smart casting, string templates, singleton handling, extensions functions. To namy a few. Coding in Java isn't an issue, I just had the privilege at Dojo of being able to implement an interoperable language into the source and too advantage of that.

MVP - MVP architecture throughout the app using the Mosby library ensures the codebase is maintainable, clean and testable.

Material design - Kept in close talks with the Play Store team to make sure the app was satisfying their requests and working closely with the CTO and UI/UX team, we made sure the app followed material design guidelines. Helping the user feel accustomed to the app the moment they start using it. Never settling until we hit 60 fps.

99.9% crash free - Cause that's how it should be.
