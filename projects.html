eTabla

Commisioned by Kuljit Bhamra and funded by Exeter University, this prototype is a fully functioning proof of concept of an Electronic Tabla instrument; to be used to help teach students the Tabla without the investment and maintenance of an authentic instrument. The team consisted of Kuljit, a product designer who created the casing and myself accounting for all the technical aspects.

Kuljit recorded samples for each note, pitch and timbre possible. An array of different force sensitive resistors trigger recorded the samples. The envelope of sound is then tweaked by a combination of factors from pressures on sensors at moment of impact and whilst resonating. As true to an authentic Tabla as possible.

A fantastic project to work on, the technical challenges were rife and brilliant. A zero latency system was vital and we got there. The logic of the sensors readings onto the sound output to emulate an authentic Tabla required great back and forther between Kuljit and I, as well as me understanding the mechanics of a Tabla.

Lists

Full stack development of a platform to add comments to your Spotify playlists to share with others.

This is a play project of mine that is mainly for use amongst me and my friends. The user will login to the platform with Spotify and be able to add comments to their playlists and tracks within. They can then share these commented playlists for their friends to see. Starting at just user playlists and comments, the relational database schema is in such a way that any Spotify playlist, track or artist can have comments from any user and open for anyone to see.

It is a great way for me to develop the full stack in one project. A backend in Node.js, Postgres database, running on Heroku rather than AWS. The API is providing data for the website and is prepped for use with Android and iOS clients once I come round to it.

Jiggy

A device to be worn on a user that tracks their activity and movements. Deciphering whether a user is sitting, standing or walking and encouraging them to have a little walkabout if they've been stationary too long.

This was a proof of concept functioning device and Android app to go to investors with. I accounted for the Android app and electronics hardware. Back in the early days of Bluetooth Low Energy beacons we made use of their tiny size, massive battery life and sensors. Device side code written in C++ tracked the relevant accelerometer and gyroscope readings, only computing when absolutely necessary to keep battery usage to a minimum and then pinging to the Android device at as greater intervals as possible. Tweaking these calculations the battery lasted weeks between charges and could decipher what the user was doing, updating the UI app side.

QuadMonitor

My first Android project, a client for the QuadCopter communication protocol I created for use with my QuadCopter. It proved very useful on the field to tweak the QuadCopter coefficient values.

I never dared use the in app remote controls and stuck to my real transmitter. In theory the remote controls worked too!

All the graphs and sliders are custom canvases. Even the text is printed to canvas so as I had total freedom over the look and feel. In reality it was my first app and I could have likely found a graphing library that would have handled all the data interpretation and drawing for me. But was a great way to get stuck in.

QuadCopter

For my final year project on my Electronic Engineering degree I chose to design and build a QuadCopter from scratch. Part of the project involved justifying all of my engineering decisions, including a full Simulink 3D model of the QuadCopter that would calculate the PID feedback coefficients to be used on the real model.
